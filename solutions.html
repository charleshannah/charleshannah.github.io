How Can You Help Stop Global Warming?


  1. 1. Power your home with renewable energy
  2. Choose a utility company that generates at least half its power from wind or solar and
  3. has been certified by Green-e Energy, an organization that vets renewable energy
  4. options. If that isn’t possible for you, take a look at your electric bill; many utilities now
  5. list other ways to support renewable sources on their monthly statements and
  6. websites.

  7. 2. Reduce water waste
  8. Saving water reduces carbon pollution, too. That's because it takes a lot of energy to
  9. pump, heat, and treat your water. So take shorter showers, turn off the tap while
  10. brushing your teeth, and switch to WaterSense-labeled fixtures and appliances.

  11. 3. Create more sustainable transportation habits
  12. Instead of ordering a private ride, do a rideshare/carpool.
  13. also avoid rapid acceleration and braking, and turn on cruise control on longer trips.
  14. Try to walk and bike to your destination whenever possible and advocate for bike
  15. lanes in your community. When buying a new car, choose a climate-friendly option

  16. 4. Advocate for a healthy planet
  17. Send a letter to party leaders and candidates telling them your vote depends on their stance
  18. on global warming.
  19. Volunteer for a candidate that supports efforts to stop global warming.
  20. Talk to friends and family about the importance of voting for climate action.

  21. 5. Eat for a climate-stable planet
  22. Eat more meat-free/plant based meals, buy organic and local whenever possible and don’t waste food.
  23. Grow your own fruits and vegetables.

  24. 6. Change lightbulbs to LEDs
  25. Quality LED lightbulbs can last 25 times longer, are more
  26. durable, and use at least 75 percent less energy than other bulbs.

  27. 7. Upcycle your furniture.
  28. From shopping cart couches to chairs from old skis, upcycled furniture can be innovative
  29. and environmentally smart. Consider using recycled materials
  30. like pallets or repurposing the furniture you already have instead of buying new.

  31. 8. Recycle your clothes
  32. Not only is fast fashion wasteful, but the environmental cost of manufacturing and
  33. distributing new clothes is devastating. A handful of retailers offer recycling programs, while
  34. companies like Patagonia will actually purchase, refurbish, and resell your gently worn garments.

  35. 9. Buy new appliances with the Energy Star label.
  36. When you need to replace a refrigerator or dishwasher, choose an appliance that’s Energy Star certified.
  37. Energy Star products are more efficient, meaning they can help lower your energy costs and reduce
  38. greenhouse gas emissions.

  39. 10. Design your workspace around natural light.
  40. Now that many people are working from home full-time due to the pandemic, reconsider your workspace:
  41. Do you need to have a light on all day? Is there an alternative spot that might get better natural light?
  42. Turning a few lights off helps you reduce electricity usage and extend the life of your lightbulbs.

  43. 11. Insulate.
  44. Simply making our homes more efficient can substantially cut the energy needed to heat and cool.
  45. Adding insulation, weather stripping, and caulking around your home can cut energy bills by more than 25 percent.

  46. 12. Downsize.
  47. Does saving the planet “spark joy?” Then follow Marie Kondo’s lead and try
  48. to be mindful of what you do and don’t need. A more measured approach to consumption
  49. can also eliminate unneeded purchases that contribute to global emissions.

  50. 13. Hack your thermostat.
  51. Simply adjust your thermostat to run 2 degrees cooler in the winter and
  52. 2 degrees warmer in the summer. You likely won’t notice much of a difference in
  53. your house, but the energy savings can be dramatic.

  54. 14. Donate to none profit organizations.
